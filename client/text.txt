<p>The <b>Bureya Range</b> (<span class="gamelink" linkto="Russian" language=""> Russian </span>: <span lang="ru">Буреинский хребет</span>, <i>Bureinskiy Khrebet</i>) is a <span class="gamelink" linkto="Mountain" range=""> mountain range </span> in the <span class="gamelink" linkto="Khabarovsk" krai=""> Khabarovsk Krai </span> and <span class="gamelink" linkto="Jewish" autonomous="" oblast=""> Jewish Autonomous Oblast </span> in the southern part of the <span class="gamelink" linkto="Russian" far="" east=""> Russian Far East </span>.
</p><p>The <span class="gamelink" linkto="Dusse-Alin" tunnel=""> Dusse-Alin Tunnel </span> on the <span class="gamelink" linkto="Baikal" amur="" mainline=""> Baikal Amur Mainline </span> crosses the range to enter the Amgun River valley. The <span class="gamelink" linkto="Bastak" nature="" reserve=""> Bastak Nature Reserve </span>, a <span class="gamelink" linkto="Protected" area=""> protected area </span>, is located in the southeastern corner of the range.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc">
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Geography">Geography</h2></div>
<p>The Bureya Range consists of a number of separate ridges with a total length of about 400&nbsp;km and with a maximum height of 2,167 metres (7,110&nbsp;ft).<sup id="cite_ref-GSЕ_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GSЕ-1"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> To the northeast it connects with the <span class="gamelink" linkto="Badzhal" range=""> Badzhal Range </span>. The range forms the <span class="gamelink" linkto="Drainage" divide=""> drainage divide </span> of the <span class="gamelink" linkto="Bureya" river=""> Bureya </span>, <span class="gamelink" linkto="Amgun" river=""> Amgun </span> and <span class="gamelink" linkto="Urmi" river=""> Urmi </span> rivers. The sources of the <span class="gamelink" linkto="Selemdzha" river=""> Selemdzha River </span> are located at the northern end, where other three ranges meet the Bureya mountain chain, the <span class="gamelink" linkto="Dusse-Alin"> Dusse-Alin </span> from the south, the <span class="gamelink" linkto="Ezop" range=""> Ezop Range </span> from the west and the <span class="gamelink" linkto="Yam-Alin"> Yam-Alin </span> from the north.<sup id="cite_ref-GЕ_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GЕ-2"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup> The Dusse-Alin and Yam-Alin are a northern prolongation of the Bureya Range.<sup id="cite_ref-GSЕ2_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GSЕ2-3"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p><p><span class="gamelink" linkto="Mount" studencheskaya=""> Mount Studencheskaya </span> is located in the southern part of the Bureya Range. At 1,421 metres (4,662&nbsp;ft), it is the highest point of the Jewish Autonomous Oblast.<sup id="cite_ref-EAO_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EAO-4"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>4<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Flora">Flora</h2></div>
<p>The slopes of the range are covered by conifer and deciduous forests.<sup id="cite_ref-GSЕ_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GSЕ-1"><span class="cite-bracket">[</span>1<span class="cite-bracket">]</span></a></sup>
</p>
